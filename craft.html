The craft

From forest to your home.

Every Ghar piece travels through hands, colour, and days of patient repetition before it reaches you.

Step by step

How block printing works.

01 — Design

The pattern is born

A motif is sketched — a vine, a bloom, a geometric repeat. Every line is deliberate.

02 — Carve

Days of carving

The design is carved into teak by hand. One pattern can take up to 7 blocks — one per colour.

03 — Print

Pressed by hand

The inked block is pressed onto the cotton, colour by colour. Each impression is a human decision.

04 — To your home

Cut, stitched, sent

Each piece is cut, stitched with contrasting piping, inspected — then sent from India to the Netherlands.

"The slight imperfections in the print are not mistakes. They are the mark of a human hand on every piece."

Our makers

Meet Karghewale —
the heart of
our collection.

Karghewale is not a factory or a supplier. It is a community of 150 weaver entrepreneurs — most of them graduates of The Handloom School — working across thirteen states of India, from Rajasthan and Gujarat to Nagaland and Arunachal Pradesh.

Each artisan entrepreneur manages their own workshop, bears the cost of their own production, and prices their own work. Karghewale exists to bring these individual makers under a shared identity — so they can reach international markets, contribute to a more sustainable fashion economy, and do so without losing agency to the middlemen who have historically cut them out of their own value chain.

Our cotton is sourced directly through Karghewale. The block printing is applied by hand, by artisans whose names we know, using techniques passed down through generations. When you buy from Ghar, the person who made your piece was not a wage worker paid per metre. They were a maker, with a stake in what they create.
