The story

Two colleagues. One shared obsession.

How a friendship in Amsterdam became a bridge to the artisan workshops of India.

Why Ghar

We started Ghar
to bring intention
home.

We wanted intentional design: good fabric that breathes, is kind to the earth, and carries a story. We wanted to know the people behind our home decor — the name of the maker, the source of the cotton — and to live with statement pieces that connect a home to something slower, more mindful, more soulful.

Ghar grew from the friendship of two Indian colleagues working together in a Montessori environment in Amsterdam. A shared love of education and Indian heritage — and the observation skills our work taught us — grew into intentional designs, made with the Karghewale artisan community in India.

"Every piece in our collection is made with love and intention. This is not a brand story. It is just what we believe."

Why cotton?

Your skin knows
the difference.

We chose thick handwoven cotton canvas because it's the most honest material we could bring into your home. It breathes, softens with every wash, and returns to the earth at the end of its life.

🌬️ Breathable

Open weave lets air circulate — no synthetic heat-trapping.

🌱 Biodegradable

Returns to earth. Zero microplastics.

💧 Better with age

Softens with every wash; colours develop a patina.

🖐️ Holds print deeply

Natural fibres absorb dye all the way through.

The people behind it

Hi, we're Dimple
& Akkriti.

We work together in Amsterdam and come from very different parts of India. What connects us: beautiful patterns and designs, good fabric, the conviction that India's artisans deserve the world's appreciation — and a great love of food. Those shared passions became Ghar: beautifully and ethically made pieces for your home. Every print begins in our drawing pads and goes through a million changes — the colour of a flower, the placement of a leaf — before it reaches our makers, and finally, you.

D

Dimple

Co-founder · Amsterdam

Works full-time in Amsterdam and builds Ghar in every hour that's left. Her background in education and livelihoods work shapes Ghar's core belief: people's work should be seen and valued. A lover of Indian food, she cooks traditional dishes with a healthy twist — and spends her evenings designing the website, making content for socials, or sketching new patterns in a park nearby.

A

Akkriti

Co-founder · Amsterdam

Also works full-time in Amsterdam, raising her son while building Ghar. A lawyer with a passion for India's early childhood education sector, she travelled across India throughout her childhood and has a fine eye for detail and good fabric. She'll be spotted deep in conversation, making an instant connection, and smiling her way into your heart.
