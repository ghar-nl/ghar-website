The story

We started Ghar
because home
deserved better.

Not better in the way of more expensive. Better in the way of more intentional. We wanted the things in our homes to have a story — to have been made by someone whose name we knew, from fabric we could trace back to its source.

Ghar grew out of a friendship between two colleagues at AMI in Amsterdam, who found themselves drawn to the same idea: that traditional crafts from Rajasthan could find a home in Amsterdam — not as exotic souvenirs, but as everyday objects worthy of a slow life.

We are both based in Amsterdam, and together we work directly with Karghewale — a community that supports, employs and upskills traditional handloom weavers and artisans across Rajasthan.

"Every piece in our collection is made with love and intention. This is not a brand story. It is just what we believe."

Our makers

Meet Karghewale —
the heart of
our collection.

The natural cotton canvas in your hands was sourced directly through Karghewale — a wonderful artisan community dedicated to supporting the livelihoods, independence, and heritage of traditional handloom weavers across Rajasthan, India.

Karghewale doesn't just source materials. They support weavers, upskill artisans, and ensure fair employment throughout the making process. Then our master block printers take over — applying each print by hand using intricate carved wooden blocks, a technique passed down through generations.

Fair wages & employment

Every artisan involved is fairly compensated. Craft is not charity — it is skilled work deserving a living wage.

Upskilling artisan communities

Karghewale actively trains and supports weavers, preserving endangered textile skills for future generations.

Transparent supply chain

We know every step from Rajasthan to your door. Because honesty is part of the product.

The craft

From forest to your sofa —
how block printing works.

A design doesn't just appear on cotton. It starts in a forest, moves through a workshop, travels through hands, colour, and days of patient repetition — before it reaches you in Amsterdam.

01 — Design

The pattern is born

A motif is chosen — a vine, a bloom, a geometric repeat — and sketched out by the artisan who will carve it. Every line is deliberate.

02 — Carve

Days of carving

The design is transferred onto teak wood and carved by hand using a small chisel. One block can take days. A single pattern uses 2 to 7 blocks — one per colour.

03 — Cotton

The cloth is prepared

100% cotton canvas is sourced, then washed and stretched flat. The fabric must be smooth and clean before a single drop of dye touches it.

04 — Ink

Block meets colour

The carved block is pressed into a colour tray — a mix of synthetic and natural dyes. Every dip must be even. Too much ink, the pattern bleeds. Too little, it fades.

05 — Print

Pressed by hand

The inked block is placed on the cloth and pressed down firmly by hand — then lifted, re-inked, and placed again. Each impression is a human decision. No machine can replicate it.

06 — Layer

Colour by colour

A second block, a second colour. Then a third. Each layer dries before the next is applied. The full pattern reveals itself slowly — up to 7 blocks building one print.

07 — Stitch

Cut, sewn, finished

The printed cloth goes to the tailoring team. Each piece is cut precisely, stitched with contrasting piping, inspected, and tagged — before it is packed and sent to Amsterdam for you.

08 — Amsterdam

Packed, sent, home

Final checks, final fold. It travels from Jaipur to Amsterdam — Dimple and Akkriti inspect every piece personally before it reaches your door.

"The slight imperfections in the print are not mistakes. They are the mark of a human hand on every piece."

Why cotton?

Your skin knows
the difference.

We chose thick handwoven cotton canvas for every piece — not just because it's beautiful to print on, but because it's the most honest material we could bring into your home.

Cotton breathes. It absorbs. It stays cool in summer, feels warm in winter, and softens with every wash. And unlike synthetic fabrics, it returns to the earth at the end of its life — no microplastics, no permanent footprint.

🌬️ Breathable

Open weave allows air circulation. No synthetic heat-trapping, no humidity buildup under your dinner plates.

🌱 Biodegradable

Returns to earth at end of life. Zero microplastics released in the wash.

💧 Gets better with age

Softens beautifully with every wash. Block print colours develop a beautiful patina over time.

🖐️ Holds print deeply

Natural fibres absorb natural dye all the way through. Block prints on cotton are built to last decades.

Care with love

Made to last.
Loved always.

🌿

Hang outside to refresh first. Fresh air does more than you think. Your piece doesn't need washing as often as you think it does.

🧊

If washing is needed: Cold water, gentle cycle, alone. No bundling with other laundry.

🚫

No bleach. No tumble drying. (We mean it. Seriously.)

☁️

Dry in the shade. Direct sunlight will fade the colours over time. Shade keeps them vibrant for years.

🔥

If you like things crisp: A slightly warm iron on the reverse side. Never directly on the print.
